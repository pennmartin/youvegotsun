Program Name: CEC - New Solar Homes Partnership
Description: The "base" incentive, which is adjusted based on expected performance, is shown here -- applies to custom homes, small developments, housing developments where solar is offered as an option, common areas of housing developments, and housing developments where solar will be installed on less than 50% of the homes. Higher incentives (beginning at $2.25/W) are offered where solar is a standard feature in a development. Higher incentives are also available for solar on affordable housing, beginning at $3.15/W. Incentives will decline as capacity targets are met.
Program Name: California Solar Initiative - PV Incentives
Description: Incentives vary by utility and sector and decline over time when certain capacity targets are met. PGE incentives as of 7/28/10 shown here. See http://www.csi-trigger.com/ for other utilities. Systems up to 5 MW are eligible, but incentives are paid on first 1 MW only. Customers w/ systems up to 30 kW may choose either the expected performance buydown or the PBI. Systems 30 kW or larger are eligible for the PBI only. Electric-displacing solar space heating/cooling and CSP are also eligible, but no data has been entered given the complicated nature of the calculation.

Program Name: California Solar Initiative - Multi-Family Affordable Solar Housing (MASH) Program
Description: This rebate is for multi-family residential housing and low-income housing. The Track 1A incentive for offsetting common area load is $1.90/watt and the Track 1B rebate for offsetting tenant load is $2.80/watt.
Program Name: California Solar Initiative - Single-Family Affordable Solar Housing (SASH) Program
Description: This program is for single-family affordable housing. In general, the household's total income must be 80% of the area median income (AMI) or less. Incentive amounts vary, depending on federal income tax liability and whether or not the household is CARE-eligible. Incentive amounts range from $4.75/watt to $7/watt. $4.75/watt is entered here. Fully subsidized systems are also available. Fully subsidized systems will be limited to systems 1.0 ? 1.2 kW, with a max incentive of $10,000. There is no stated maximum for partially subsidized systems.
Program Name: Business Energy Investment Tax Credit (ITC)
Description: The American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 allows taxpayers eligible for the federal renewable electricity production tax credit (PTC) to take the federal business energy investment tax credit (ITC) instead of taking the PTC for new installations.
